City of Burlington, Meet Your Civic Hackers

{{ page.description }}

Civic hacking is a participatory effort to improve communities through technology. Like government, civic hacking is most effective when it is open and transparent. Code for BTV is a Code for America Brigade that develops, deploys, and maintains modern civic software and infrastructure for the benefit of communities in the greater Burlington area. Code for BTV works with the City of Burlington to develop civic apps that can facilitate local services, encourage civic engagement, or otherwise benefit the local community. The conversations that occur at Office Hours are instrumental in informing the work of civic hackers.

All are welcome to participate in Code for BTV Office Hours at City Hall, providing that they do so in a manner that contributes to a constructive process.

  • Elected officials and government staff are encouraged to attend to discuss the opportunities and challenges that exist in their work.
  • Civic hackers are encouraged to communicate directly with those knowledgeable about the opportunities and challenges faced by our community.
  • The general public is welcome—and encouraged—to participate in the work being done by Code for BTV.

There is no need to register or RSVP to attend.
